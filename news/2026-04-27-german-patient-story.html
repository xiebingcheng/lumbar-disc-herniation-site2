German Patient Shares Spinal Stenosis Surgery Experience

April 27, 2026 · Patient Story

Klaus Weber, a 58-year-old engineer from Munich, traveled to Shanghai for spinal stenosis surgery after a 7-month wait through the German public system. He shares his experience and the factors that led him to choose medical tourism.

The Decision Process

"My neurologist said I needed surgery within 6 months to prevent permanent nerve damage. The earliest appointment I could get in Germany was in 8 months. After researching alternatives, I found myMedVia and arranged everything within 3 weeks."

The Experience

"The hospital was impressive—better equipped than most German hospitals I've seen. Dr. Chen spoke excellent German, and they even had a German translator for the first few days. The laminoplasty procedure took 4 hours, and I was walking with a walker the next morning."

Outcome

Three months post-surgery, Klaus reports being completely pain-free and has returned to his normal activities, including cycling. "The total cost was about €6,000 including accommodation and transfers. In Germany, the same procedure would have been €25,000 with my public insurance covering only a portion."
