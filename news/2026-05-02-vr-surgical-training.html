Virtual Reality in Surgical Training: China's Medical Innovation

May 2, 2026 · Innovation

Chinese medical institutions are pioneering the use of virtual reality simulation in spine surgeon training, allowing trainees to practice complex procedures thousands of times before operating on patients. This technology is now standard in all our partner hospitals.

How VR Training Works

The system uses patient-specific anatomy from CT scans to create realistic 3D models. Trainees use haptic feedback controllers to practice discectomy, fusion, and decompression procedures in a risk-free environment.

Training Results

Surgeons who trained with VR simulation showed 34% fewer complications in their first 50 real procedures compared to traditionally trained peers. Each surgeon completes over 200 hours of VR practice before performing independent surgery.
