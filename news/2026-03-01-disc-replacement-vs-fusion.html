Artificial Disc Replacement vs Spinal Fusion: Long-Term Outcomes

March 1, 2026 · Treatment

For patients requiring disc removal, choosing between artificial disc replacement (ADR) and spinal fusion represents one of the most important treatment decisions. Understanding the long-term implications helps make an informed choice.

Artificial Disc Replacement

ADR preserves motion at the treated level. 10-year data shows 85% of patients maintain functional mobility. Best for single-level disease in younger patients without significant facet joint arthritis.

Spinal Fusion

Fusion eliminates motion but provides excellent pain relief. 10-year data shows 90%+ fusion rates with good outcomes. May increase stress on adjacent discs over time.

Making the Choice

Our surgeons consider age, activity level, number of affected levels, and presence of adjacent disc disease when recommending the optimal approach for each patient.
