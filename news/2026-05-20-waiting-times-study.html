Spine Surgery Waiting Times: Global Comparison Study

May 20, 2026 · Research

A comprehensive international study analyzing spine surgery waiting times across 15 countries reveals that patients in Canada and the UK face wait times up to 12 times longer than those in China, prompting many to consider medical tourism as a viable alternative.

Key Findings

The study, conducted by the International Health Policy Institute, analyzed waiting times for elective spine surgeries across developed nations. The results showed dramatic variations depending on geographic location and healthcare system.

  • Canada: Average wait time of 11.2 months for non-emergency spine surgery
  • United Kingdom: 9.8 months average wait time
  • Australia: 6.4 months average wait time
  • United States: 3.2 months (with insurance complications)
  • China: 5-7 days for international patients

Impact on Patient Outcomes

Extended waiting times have been linked to worse outcomes in spine surgery patients. Dr. James Morrison, orthopedic surgeon at Toronto General, notes that "delayed surgical intervention for disc herniation can lead to permanent nerve damage, chronic pain syndromes, and reduced quality of life."

Medical Tourism as Alternative

With flights to major Chinese medical hubs taking 12-15 hours from North America and all-inclusive surgical packages available, patients are increasingly viewing medical tourism as a practical solution to avoid prolonged suffering.
