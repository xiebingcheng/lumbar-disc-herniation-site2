Shanghai Hospital Receives JCI Reaccreditation

Peking Union Medical College Hospital has received its third Joint Commission International reaccreditation, maintaining the highest standards of patient safety and quality care for international patients.

What JCI Accreditation Means

Joint Commission International (JCI) is the global leader in healthcare quality evaluation and accreditation. JCI standards are recognized worldwide as the benchmark for patient safety and quality of care in healthcare organizations.

The Accreditation Process

The reaccreditation involved a comprehensive review spanning multiple areas:

  • International Patient Safety Goals: Proper identification, communication, medication safety
  • Care Standards: Assessment, anesthesia, surgical care, medication management
  • Facility Standards: Infection control, governance, staff qualifications
  • Quality Improvement: Performance measurement, continuing education

Key Achievements in the Review

  • 100% compliance with international patient safety goals
  • Zero healthcare-associated infections in the spine surgery unit over 18 months
  • 98.5% patient satisfaction among international patients
  • 24/7 interpreter services available in 12 languages

Statement from Hospital Leadership

"This third reaccreditation confirms our commitment to providing world-class healthcare to every patient who walks through our doors. We are proud to offer international patients the same standard of care they would receive at leading institutions in their home countries."

What This Means for International Patients

For medical tourists considering treatment in China, JCI accreditation provides assurance that:

  • Clinical staff meet rigorous international training standards
  • Patient safety protocols follow globally recognized best practices
  • Medical records and communication meet international quality benchmarks
  • Quality improvement processes are continuously monitored and evaluated
