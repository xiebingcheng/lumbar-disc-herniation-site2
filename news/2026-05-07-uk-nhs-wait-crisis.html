UK NHS Announces 18-Month Spine Surgery Wait Crisis

May 7, 2026 · Industry

The UK National Health Service has reported that orthopedic spine surgeries are now facing waits of up to 18 months in some regions, citing surgical backlogs and staffing shortages. Patients are increasingly seeking private alternatives or medical tourism options.

Regional Variations

Wait times vary significantly across the UK: London averages 14 months, while Northern England and Scotland see waits of 16-18 months. Elective spine procedures are prioritized below emergency and cancer surgeries.

Patient Alternatives

For patients unwilling to wait, options include private UK surgery (typically £15,000-£30,000) or medical tourism to China with all-inclusive packages starting at $4,500, saving up to 80% while receiving comparable quality care.
