Patient Story: From Chronic Pain to Mountain Hiking in 8 Weeks

May 4, 2026 · Patient Story

Sarah Mitchell, a 42-year-old software engineer from Denver, spent three years managing chronic back pain before deciding on spine surgery. After researching options, she traveled to Guangzhou for endoscopic discectomy. Just 8 weeks later, she completed a 12-kilometer mountain hike.

Sarah's Journey

"I tried everything—physical therapy, injections, medications. Nothing provided lasting relief. When my doctor said I'd need surgery, the wait in the US was 4 months and my insurance had a $9,000 deductible. A colleague who'd had surgery in China recommended myMedVia, and I was operated on within 2 weeks."

The Procedure

The endoscopic discectomy was performed by Dr. Liu at Guangzhou United Hospital. "I was discharged the same day, back to my hotel. The minimal incision meant I was moving around the next day with minimal pain," Sarah recalls.

Results

Eight weeks post-surgery, Sarah completed the Grays Peak trail in Colorado—something she hadn't attempted in over three years. "I feel like I got my life back," she says.
