Canadian Patient Returns to Active Lifestyle After Spinal Surgery

After 18 months of chronic back pain and a 9-month wait for surgery in Canada, Michael Thompson traveled to Beijing and underwent a successful microdiscectomy. He is now back to hiking and playing with his grandchildren.

Michael's Journey

For 58-year-old Michael Thompson from Ontario, the simplest activities became impossible. "I couldn't play with my grandkids without paying for it for days afterward," he recalls. "Even standing for more than 10 minutes was agony."

After an MRI confirmed a severe lumbar disc herniation compressing his sciatic nerve, Michael was told he would need to wait 9 months for surgery through the public healthcare system. "The pain was affecting my work and my family life. Nine months felt like forever."

Making the Decision

Through a colleague whose mother had successful spine surgery in China, Michael learned about medical tourism options. "I was skeptical at first. But after researching and speaking with the medical coordinator at myMedVia, I felt confident. They answered every question and provided detailed information about the hospital and surgeon."

The Treatment Experience

Michael's journey to Beijing took less than 3 weeks from his initial inquiry. "The hospital was modern and clean—actually nicer than some hospitals back home. The surgeon was excellent and spoke fluent English. They even had a translator available 24/7."

The microdiscectomy procedure took 45 minutes under general anesthesia. "I was up walking the same day," Michael says. "After 3 days in the hospital, I moved to a recovery apartment near the hospital for another 4 days of monitoring."

Recovery and Results

Six weeks after surgery, Michael is virtually pain-free. "I went from being unable to stand for 10 minutes to hiking 5 kilometers with my grandchildren. I wish I'd done it sooner."

Cost Comparison

Michael's total expenses in China, including surgery, hospital stay, accommodation, and airport transfers, came to approximately $4,800 CAD. In Canada, he was quoted $28,000 for the same procedure through private healthcare, and his public system wait was 9 months.

Advice for Other Patients

"Don't let fear hold you back. The medical team in China was professional, caring, and the quality of care exceeded my expectations. For anyone facing a long wait for spine surgery, medical tourism is a viable option worth considering."
