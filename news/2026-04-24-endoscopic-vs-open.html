Endoscopic vs Open Surgery: Which Is Right for You?

April 24, 2026 · Treatment

When considering spine surgery, patients often face a choice between traditional open surgery and newer endoscopic techniques. Understanding the differences can help you make an informed decision about your treatment.

Open Microdiscectomy

Traditional approach with a 2-3 inch incision. The surgeon directly visualizes the disc and nerves. Best for complex cases or revision surgeries. Recovery: 6-12 weeks.

Endoscopic Discectomy

Minimally invasive with a 7-10mm incision. Uses a camera and specialized instruments through a tube. Less tissue damage, faster recovery. Best for straightforward herniations. Recovery: 2-4 weeks.

Which Is Better?

The answer depends on your specific condition. Our surgeons will recommend the approach that offers the best outcome for your particular anatomy and pathology. Contact us for a personalized assessment.
